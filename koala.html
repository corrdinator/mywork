KOALA

Koalas are tree-dwelling marsupials native to Australia, easily recognized by their fluffy ears, large noses, and cuddly appearance. Though often called "koala bears," they are not bears at all.

They spend most of their time sleeping—up to 20 hours a day—due to their low-energy diet of eucalyptus leaves. Koalas are mostly solitary and live in eucalyptus forests, where they climb and leap from tree to tree using strong limbs and sharp claws.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM:

The koalas are in pen K07, tucked away in the shaded eucalyptus grove near the picnic area.
